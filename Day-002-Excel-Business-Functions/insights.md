# 📈 Business Insights

## Project Summary

The sales data was analyzed using Excel's logical and conditional functions to classify transactions and generate key business metrics.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. High Sale Orders are Limited

Only **1,162** out of **9,977** orders were classified as **High Sale**, indicating that high-value transactions are relatively infrequent.

---

### 2. Most Orders are Low Sale

A total of **8,815** orders were classified as **Low Sale**, showing that customers generally place lower-value orders.

---

### 3. High Sale Orders Generate Significant Revenue

Although High Sale orders represent a smaller portion of total transactions, they generated approximately **1,474,984.10** in sales revenue, making them highly valuable to the business.

---

### 4. Average High Sale Value is Strong

The average value of High Sale transactions is **1,269.35**, which is well above the defined business threshold of **500**.

---

### 5. Automation Improves Efficiency

Using Excel functions such as **IF**, **COUNTIF**, **SUMIF**, and **AVERAGEIF** eliminated the need for manual calculations and enabled faster, more accurate reporting.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Increase the number of High Sale orders through product bundles and upselling strategies.
- Identify products that frequently appear in High Sale transactions and prioritize their promotion.
- Monitor Low Sale orders to identify opportunities for increasing average order value.
- Continue using automated Excel reports to improve reporting speed and accuracy.
